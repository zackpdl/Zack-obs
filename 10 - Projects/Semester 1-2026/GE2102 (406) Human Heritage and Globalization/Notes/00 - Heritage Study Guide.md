## 1. Key Concepts & Definitions

### History
- **History** (Greek *historia* = inquiry/study of events) — reconstruction of human activities in the past, neither fact nor fiction.
- **Herodotus** (5th c. BC) — Greek historian, considered father of history; used archives and scientific investigation.

### Civilization
- Focus on developments of societies and cultures.
- **"City"** is the origin and center of human activities.
- **Aspects of civilization:** Geography, Society, Politics, Economy, Culture, Intellectual achievement, Artistic achievement.

### Heritage
- **Cultural properties** passing down from generation to generation.
- **Tangible culture:** artefacts, historical buildings.
- **Intangible culture:** beliefs, norms, ceremonies, activities.

### Source Materials for Study
- **Archaeological artifacts:** fossils, bones, remains of tools and buildings.
- **Historical artifacts:** written accounts, scriptures, documents, paintings.
- **Primary source:** authentic/made at the time of event (e.g., Rosetta Stone 200 BC, Vinaya Pitaka).
- **Secondary source:** made later based on primary sources (e.g., decipherment of Rosetta Stone, Abhidhamma Pitaka).

### Timeline of World History
| Event | Time |
|-------|------|
| Emergence of Planet Earth | 4.5 billion years ago |
| Emergence of human species | 3 million years ago |
| Emergence of agriculture | 10,000 years ago |
| Emergence of civilization | ~3000 BC |

### Periods
- **Prehistoric:** Before invention of writing (3 million yrs — 3000 BC)
- **Ancient:** 3000 BC — 5th c. AD
- **Medieval:** 5th — 15th c.
- **Early Modern:** 16th — 17th c.
- **Late Modern:** 18th — 19th c.
- **Contemporary:** Late 20th — 21st c.

## 2. Prehistory

### Human Evolution Timeline (Paleolithic)

| Species | Time | Key Traits |
|---------|------|------------|
| Australopithecine | 4 million yrs ago | Bipedal primates |
| Homo habilis | 2 million yrs | "Man with dexterity", Olduvai Gorge (Tanzania), first stone tools |
| Homo erectus | 500,000 yrs | Upright spine, fire use, better tools (hand axe, spear), Peking Man, Java Man |
| Neanderthals | 300,000 yrs | Link between H. erectus and H. sapiens, burial practices |
| Homo sapiens | 200,000 yrs | "Wise man", direct ancestors of modern humans |
| Cro-Magnons | Late Paleolithic | Survived Ice Age, direct ancestors of modern humans |

### Physical Features of Humans (Pros)
- **Dexterity:** use of hands and fingers
- **Speech organs:** ability to communicate complex ideas
- **Cognitive skills:** larger brain capacity
- **Omnivores:** eat varied food (plant & meat)

### Physical Features of Humans (Cons)
- Vulnerable babies (dependent for ~3 years)
- Physically weaker than most wild animals
- Prone to mental sickness, aggression, war
- Consume more resources, cause environmental destruction

### Paleolithic Period

**Early Paleolithic:**
- **Way of life:** Scavenging, foraging, hunting; small family groups; nomadic; caves/trees; teamwork
- **Beliefs:** Fear of predators and natural phenomena
- **Achievements:** Tools from bone/wood/stone (hand axe), fire for warmth/cooking/protection

**Ice Ages (2 million – 11,000 years ago):**
- Extreme climate change, ice sheets covered northern hemisphere
- Natural selection, extinction of many species
- Human migration to find food and warmer places

**Late Paleolithic:**
- Only Homo sapiens survived the Ice Age
- Evolution of speech → spoken language → passing knowledge to next generation
- **Beliefs:** Supernatural, fertility cults, tomb burial (afterlife), animism, shamanism, totemism, hunting rites, sympathetic magic
- **Achievements:** Tools with handles, cutting/drill tools, fishing hooks, bows & arrows, sewing, amulets; cave paintings (Lascaux, Altamira); Venus of Willendorf

### Mesolithic Period (~10,000 years ago)
- **Transitional** period from Paleolithic to Neolithic
- More invented tools: flint, fishing tools, early boats, bows & arrows, huts, pottery
- More sedentary lifestyle, settlements near water
- New diets: shellfish, edible grasses (rice cultivation)
- Animal domestication: cattle, pig, horse, sheep
- Jomon pottery (Japan)

### Neolithic Period (~8,000 years ago)
- **Neolithic Revolution / Agricultural Revolution** — beginning of agriculture
- Land cultivation, animal domestication, food storage → higher survival rate
- Examples: Jericho (Jordan), Catalhoyuk (Turkey)
- **Way of life:** Sedentary, systematic; population increase; social organization (land ownership, social hierarchy); division of labor (men = outdoor/dangerous, women = household); patriarchy
- **Beliefs:** Complex funeral rites, myths (polytheism), worship of nature/sun/moon/rivers, Mother Earth, astrology
- **Achievements:** Knowledge of weather/water management; crops (rice, wheat, barley, flax, corn); animal domestication; specialized tools; spinning wheel pottery; metallurgy; early houses; **Stonehenge** (megalithic post-and-lintel)

### Metal Ages
- **Copper Age** (5000 BC) — copper smelting
- **Bronze Age** (3000 BC) — alloy of copper + tin
- **Iron Age** (1200 BC) — early iron tools in Anatolia

### Key Vocabulary — Prehistory
- **Paleolithic:** Old Stone Age
- **Mesolithic:** Middle Stone Age
- **Neolithic:** New Stone Age
- **Hominin:** Bipedal primates
- **Animism:** Worship of spirits in nature
- **Shamanism:** Person who contacts spirits
- **Totemism:** Objects believed to have magical power
- **Sympathetic magic:** Ritual to influence events by imitation
- **Megalith:** Large stone construction
- **Nomadic:** Roaming, no fixed settlement
- **Sedentary:** Permanent settlement

## 3. Ancient Mesopotamia

### Geography
- "Land between two rivers" (Tigris & Euphrates) — the **Fertile Crescent**
- Located in north of Arabia (modern Iraq)
- Alluvial silt deposits; irregular flood and drought → irrigation technology developed
- Open land, fewer natural barriers → vulnerable to invasion

### Timeline of Peoples (3000–500 BC)

| People | Period | Key Contributions |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **1. Sumerians** | First settlers | Agriculture, irrigation, **Cuneiform** writing, ziggurats, city-states, polytheism, Epic of Gilgamesh, priest-kings (theocracy) |
| **2. Akkadians** | ~2300 BC | King Sargon united Akkad & Sumeria → more centralized state |
| **3. Amorites (Old Babylonia)** | ~2000 BC | Babylon as capital; **King Hammurabi** (1792–1750 BC) — world's first code of law |
| **4. Hittites** | ~1595 BC | Indo-European; invaded Babylonia; iron smelting; horse-drawn chariot; capital Hattusa |
| **5. Kassites** | After Hittites | "Dark Age" of Mesopotamia; political unrest |
| **6. Phoenicians** | Sea Peoples | Maritime trade; phonetic alphabet → forerunner of Greek/Western scripts |
| **7. Assyrians** | ~1300 BC | Restored kingdom; warmongers; King Sennacherib (capital Nineveh); King Ashurbanipal |
| **8. Chaldeans (New Babylonia)** | ~612 BC | King Nebuchadnezzar; Babylonian Captivity of Jews; Hanging Gardens |
| **9. Persians** | 600–530 BC | King Cyrus the Great freed Hebrews; King Darius & Xerxes vs. Greece; **Zoroastrianism** (monotheism) influenced Judaism & Christianity |
| **10. Hebrews (Jews/Israelites)** | ~1000 BC | **Judaism** (monotheism, Yahweh); Torah; Moses (Exodus); Kings Saul, David, Solomon; Queen of Sheba |

### Society & Politics
- Early: Independent city-states (Ur, Uruk, Lagash) ruled by **priest-kings (Gudea)** → theocracy
- Later: Centralized kingdom ruled by **warrior-kings (Lugal)**
- Temple-centered communities → gods' favor = good harvests

### Religion
- **Polytheism:** Gods personify nature — Ishtar (fertility), Anu (sky), Enlil (wind), Enki (water)
- Each city had a patron god (e.g., Marduk in Babylon, Innanna in Ur)
- **Epic of Gilgamesh:** pessimistic view of life, search for immortality, reflects beliefs relevant to Judaism, Christianity, Islam (Noah/Flood)

### Intellectual Achievements
- **Irrigation:** Canals, ditches, reservoirs, Archimedean screw
- **Seeder plough**
- **Wheel:** Two-wheeled chariot, four-wheeled cart, horse-drawn chariot
- **Cuneiform:** World's oldest writing system (wedge-shaped on clay tablets)
- **Literature:** *Epic of Gilgamesh* — possibly world's first literature
- **Law:** **Hammurabi's Code** (282 laws) — "an eye for an eye" — first written law
- **Astronomy:** Lunar calendar, time division by 60/12 (sexagesimal system)
- **Mathematics:** Division, multiplication, sexagesimal units, geometry
- **Music:** Lyre, flute, drum; melody (unison, octave)
- **Architecture:** Ziggurats (stepped-temples), city walls with arch/vault, Ishtar Gate, Hanging Gardens, Tower of Babel

### Key Vocabulary — Mesopotamia
- **Cuneiform:** Wedge-shaped writing (Latin *cuneus* = wedge)
- **Ziggurat:** Stepped temple, abode of gods on Earth
- **Theocracy:** Government by priests/divine authority
- **Polytheism:** Belief in many gods
- **Fertile Crescent:** Fertile arc from Mesopotamia to Mediterranean
- **Semitic:** Language family including Hebrews, Arabs, Phoenicians
- **Stela:** Stone slab with inscriptions (e.g., Hammurabi's Code)
- **Bas-relief:** Sculpture where figures project slightly from background

## 4. Ancient Egypt

### Geography
- Northeast Africa; "Egypt is the gift of the Nile"
- **Nile River** — world's longest, annual flood brings fertility
- Natural barriers deter invasion → political stability
- **Lower Egypt** = Nile delta (north); **Upper Egypt** = plateau/south
- Abundant resources → developed economy and industries

### Timeline

| Period | Dates | Key Events |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Predynastic** | ~3000–2686 BC | Villages (Nomes) unified; Pharaoh Menes (Narmer) unified two kingdoms |
| **Old Kingdom** | 2686–2160 BC | Capital Memphis; absolute monarchy; Pharaoh as divine ruler; **pyramid building** (Zoser's step pyramid by Imhotep; Khufu's Great Pyramid at Giza); declined due to unrest/over-taxation |
| **First Intermediate** | 2160–2055 BC | Political unrest |
| **Middle Kingdom** | 2055–1650 BC | Capital Thebes; **Golden Age** of trade & intellect; public works (temples, granaries, drainage); declined by Hyksos invasion |
| **Second Intermediate** | 1650–1550 BC | Hyksos rule |
| **New Kingdom** | 1550–1069 BC | Pharaoh Ahmose I restored Egypt; military expansion; Akhenaten's monotheistic reform (cult of Aten); Tutankhamun restored Amun; Rameses II militarism; Abu Simbel |

### Key Pharaohs & Figures
| Pharaoh | Period | Significance |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| **Menes (Narmer)** | Predynastic | Unified Upper & Lower Egypt |
| **Zoser (Djoser)** | Old Kingdom | First pyramid (Step Pyramid, Saqqara) — architect **Imhotep** |
| **Sneferu** | Old Kingdom | Bent Pyramid, Dahshur |
| **Khufu (Cheops)** | Old Kingdom | Great Pyramid of Giza |
| **Amenemhet I** | Middle Kingdom | Restored order, moved capital to Thebes |
| **Ahmose I** | New Kingdom | Expelled Hyksos, began 18th dynasty |
| **Hatshepsut** | New Kingdom | Female pharaoh, military expedition |
| **Akhenaten** | New Kingdom | Monotheistic reform — cult of Aten; with wife **Nefertiti** |
| **Tutankhamun** | New Kingdom | Restored Amun; famous tomb discovered by Howard Carter (1923) |
| **Rameses II** | New Kingdom | Militarism, Abu Simbel temples |
| **Cleopatra VII** | Ptolemaic | Last native ruler; after her death → Roman rule |

### Society & Politics
- **Absolute monarchy** — Pharaoh as divine ruler (associated with Horus)
- **Endogamy** to keep royal blood pure
- **Administration:** Viziers (nobles), Nomarchs (local governors), Scribes
- **Social pyramid:** Pharaoh → nobles/priests → scribes/merchants → farmers → slaves

### Religion
- **Polytheism:** Nature is sacred; half-human half-animal gods
- Strong belief in **afterlife** and **resurrection**
- **Cult of Osiris** (lord of underworld) and **Isis** (goddess of Nile)
- Pharaoh associated with **Horus** (sky god), son of Osiris, son of **Ra** (sun god)
- **Mummification** — preservation of body for afterlife
- **Book of the Dead** — judgment scene, weighing of heart

### Intellectual Achievements
- **Astronomy:** Egyptian calendar based on Nile flood + Sirius (Dog Star)
- **Mathematics/Geometry:** Pyramid construction shows advanced engineering
- **Medicine:** Herbs (apothecary), surgery, dentistry, mummification
- **Ship building:** Papyrus boats, wooden boats bound with cords
- **Nilometer** — measured Nile water levels

### Writing & Literature
- **Hieroglyphics:** Pictographic, formal/ritualistic script
- **Hieratic:** Informal, more phonetic
- **Papyrus:** Invention of paper from papyrus plant
- **Rosetta Stone** (discovered 1799 during Napoleon's invasion) — key to deciphering Egyptian writing
- Major texts: *Book of the Dead*, Coffin Texts

### Artistic Achievements
- Inspired by religion; grand monuments: **Pyramids, Sphinx, Temples**
- Bas-reliefs, mural paintings with multicolors
- Sculpture: geometrical, symmetrical, calm expression
- Metallurgy: copper, bronze, gold with ivory/precious stones

### Key Vocabulary — Egypt
- **Pharaoh:** Divine ruler of Egypt
- **Mastaba:** Early flat-topped tomb
- **Hieroglyphics:** Sacred picture-writing
- **Papyrus:** Early paper made from reeds
- **Mummification:** Embalming process for afterlife
- **Vizier:** High-ranking royal official
- **Nomarch:** Provincial governor
- **Sphinx:** Mythical creature (lion body + human head)
- **Obelisk:** Tall four-sided stone pillar

## 5. Ancient India

### Geography (3 Regions)
1. **Northern (Hindustan):** Mountainous, river valleys (Indus, Ganges, Yamuna, Brahmaputra); cultural/political center
2. **Central (Deccan):** Plateau, arid, forests; Godavari River
3. **Southern (Tamil):** Tropical, rainforest, hills; Kaveri River

### Peoples
- **Dravidians:** Indigenous, dark complexion, Dravidian dialects (Tamil, Malayalam)
- **Aryans:** Migrated from Central Asia, lighter complexion, Indo-European dialects (Sanskrit, Pali, Hindi)

### Timeline

| Period | Dates | Key Events |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Ancient (Dravidian)** | | |
| Indus Civilization | ~2500 BC | Mohenjo-Daro, Harappa; grid-pattern cities; underground sewage |
| Aryan Invasion | ~2000 BC | Based on Vedas |
| **Classical (Aryan)** | | |
| Vedic Period | ~1500 BC | Early kingdoms, Vedas composed |
| Greek Invasion | 326 BC | Alexander the Great |
| Maurya Dynasty | 322 BC | First Indian empire; King Ashoka → Buddhism |
| Kushan Dynasty | ~30 BC | Trade with China; Gandhara art |
| Gupta Dynasty | ~200 CE | Golden Age; King Chandragupta II; Fa Xian records |
| Harsha Dynasty | 606 CE | Decline, Arab invasion |
| **Medieval (Muslim)** | 738–1556 CE | Delhi Sultanate, Mughal Rule |
| **Modern (British)** | 1858–1947 | British Raj; Independence 1947 |

### Indus Valley Civilization (Harappan)
- Contemporary to Mesopotamia; sites in Pakistan
- **Beliefs:** Polytheism, animism, water spirit, animal respect
- **Politics:** Theocracy (priest-king), peaceful agrarian communities
- **Language:** Pictographic script (still undeciphered)
- **Achievements:** Grid-pattern cities, baked-brick buildings, underground sewage, Great Bath, mathematics, geometry, pottery, metallurgy
- **Decline:** River course change, drought, Aryan invasion

### Aryan Civilization

**Vedic Period (~1500 BC):**
- City-states along Ganges (Kosala, Magadha)
- Vedas written and compiled
- **Caste System (Varna):**
  1. **Brahmins** (priests/teachers) — from god's head
  2. **Kshatriyas** (warriors/rulers) — from god's hands
  3. **Vaishyas** (merchants/farmers) — from god's legs
  4. **Shudras** (servants/laborers) — from god's feet
  - Below: **Dalits** (untouchables/outcasts)
- **Pros of caste:** Practical organization, skill development
- **Cons:** Racism, discrimination, human rights violations

### Major Dynasties
1. **Maurya** (322 BC): Chandragupta Maurya → King **Ashoka** (supported Buddhism, disseminated it across Asia); Pillar of Sarnath (Lion Capital — now India's national emblem)
2. **Kushan** (~30 BC): Trade between Asia & Europe; King Kaniska; Mahayana Buddhism; **Gandhara art** (Greek-influenced Buddha images)
3. **Gupta** (~200 CE): **Golden Age**; King Samudragupta & Chandragupta II; peaceful, civilized society (recorded by Chinese monk Fa Xian); Gupta Buddha style (symmetrical, wet-look drapery, half-open eyes)

### Indian Religions

**Brahmanism:** Early Aryan belief; polytheism (Indra, Varuna, Agni); prayer + sacrifice; Brahmins as intermediaries

**Hinduism:** Evolved from Brahmanism (Upanishads); less ritualistic, more philosophical
- Key concepts: **Atman** (individual soul), **Brahman** (absolute soul), **Maya** (illusion), **Moksha** (salvation/union with Brahman), **Samsara** (reincarnation), **Dharma** (duty), **Karma** (moral consequence)
- **Trimurti:** Brahma (creator), Vishnu (protector), Shiva (destroyer)
- Sacred texts: Vedas, Upanishads, Mahabharata, Ramayana, Bhagavad Gita

**Jainism:** Founded by **Mahavira**; Ahimsa (non-violence), Anekantavada (open-mindedness), Aparigraha (detachment)

**Buddhism:** Prince **Siddhartha** (the Buddha = Enlightened One)
- Contrast to Hinduism: No sacrifice, denies caste system
- **Tripitaka** (Three Baskets) — Buddhist texts
- **Four Noble Truths:** Dukkha (suffering), Samudaya (cause), Nirodha (end), Magga (path)
- **Eightfold Path:** Right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration
- **Sects:** Theravada (southeast Asia), Mahayana (east Asia — Bodhisattva concept)
- King Ashoka's reign → Buddhism spread to Sri Lanka, China, SE Asia

### Intellectual Achievements
- **Hindu-Arabic numeral system** (invented by Aryabhata, 5th c.) — concept of **zero**, place-value system → revolutionized mathematics worldwide
- **Ashram system** (ideal life stages): Brahmacharya (student) → Grihastha (householder) → Vanaprastha (retirement) → Sannyasa (renunciation)
- **Secular knowledge:** Kamashastra (pleasure), Arthashastra (wealth), Dharmashastra (truth/morality)

### Artistic Achievements
- **Cave temples:** Ajanta (Buddhist), Elephanta (Hindu), Ellora (multi-faith), Sigiriya (Sri Lanka)
- **Stupas:** Burial mounds for relics (Sanchi)
- **Hindu temples:** Shikhara (northern style), Vimana (southern style) — e.g., Khajuraho, Meenakshi
- **Literature:** Vedas, Upanishads, Mahabharata (longest epic), Ramayana, works of Kalidasa
- **Music, dance, drama:** Inspired by religion; Shiva Nataraja (cosmic dancer)

### Key Vocabulary — India
- **Vedas:** Sacred knowledge texts in Sanskrit
- **Caste (Varna):** Social hierarchy based on birth
- **Dalits:** "Untouchables" outside caste system
- **Moksha:** Spiritual liberation
- **Samsara:** Cycle of rebirth
- **Karma:** Moral cause and effect
- **Dharma:** Divine order/duty
- **Ahimsa:** Non-violence
- **Nirvana:** End of suffering (Buddhist salvation)
- **Stupa:** Buddhist burial mound
- **Bodhisattva:** Enlightened being who delays nirvana to help others

## 6. Ancient China

### Geography
- **Eastern region:** Fertile river valleys — Huang He (Yellow River — cultural/political center) and Chang Jiang (Yangtze — agriculture/trade)
- **Western region:** Arid, mountainous (Tibetan Plateau, Gobi Desert) — less populated, ethnic minorities
- Fourth largest country; abundant resources; far from other ancient civilizations → unique culture

### Timeline

| Period | Dates | Key Events |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Prehistoric** | | |
| Paleolithic | 700,000 yrs ago | Peking Man (Homo erectus) — Zhoukoudian |
| Neolithic | 9000 BC | Yangshao (painted pottery), Longshan (black pottery), Sanxingdui |
| **Ancient China** | | |
| Xia Dynasty (legendary) | ~3000 BC | King Yu stopped flood; Erlitou culture; hereditary rule began |
| Shang Dynasty | ~1700 BC | First historical dynasty; oracle bones; bronze; human sacrifice |
| Zhou Dynasty | ~1100 BC | Mandate of Heaven; feudal society; **Hundred Schools of Thought** |
| **Imperial China** | | |
| Qin Dynasty | 221 BC | First empire; **Qin Shi Huang**; Great Wall; Terracotta Army; Legalism |
| Han Dynasty | 206 BC | Cultural identity; Silk Road; Confucianism restored; imperial exams |
| Sui Dynasty | 581 CE | Grand Canal; Buddhism spread |
| Tang Dynasty | 618 CE | Cosmopolitan; state Buddhism; Wu Zetian (only empress) |
| Song Dynasty | 960 CE | Overseas trade; landscape painting; printing |
| **Medieval China** | | |
| Yuan (Mongol) | 1260 CE | Kublai Khan; Marco Polo |
| Ming Dynasty | 1368 CE | Han revival; Forbidden City; Zheng He expeditions |
| Qing (Manchu) | 1644 CE | Last dynasty; western imperialism |
| **Modern China** | 1912–1949 | Republic → People's Republic |

### Ancient Dynasties — Key Details

**Xia (Legendary):** King Yu tamed the flood; first hereditary rule; Erlitou culture (bronze)

**Shang (1700–1100 BC):**
- Capital: Yin (Anyang)
- **Oracle bones** — earliest Chinese writing (pictographic/ideographic)
- Bronze metallurgy (vessels, weapons); jade ornaments; human sacrifice
- Tomb of Fu Hao

**Zhou (1100–221 BC):**
- **Mandate of Heaven** — ruler chosen by Heaven; if ruler misbehaves, Heaven gives mandate to another
- Longest dynasty (~800 years); Western Zhou (Haojing) → Eastern Zhou (Luoyang)
- **Feudal society** — king appointed dukes to govern provinces
- **Spring and Autumn / Warring States periods** — civil wars
- **Hundred Schools of Thought** (Chinese philosophy emerges)

### Chinese Philosophy

**Confucianism** (Kongzi / Confucius, ~500 BC):
- Emphasis on **virtues** and **education**
- **Five Relationships:** Parent–child (filial piety), Ruler–subject (loyalty), Husband–wife (love), Elder–younger sibling (care), Friend–friend (sincerity)
- **Ren (Humanity):** Gratitude as fundamental virtue
- **Junzi (Noble Man):** Cultivate self through learning and moral conduct
- **Li (Rite):** Proper behavior through ritual
- Criticized as conservative / obstacle to modernization
- **Five Classics:** Book of Change, Poetry, Rites, History, Annals

**Daoism / Taoism** (Laozi / Lao Tzu):
- Balance, harmony with nature
- Society's rules corrupt human nature → retreat to nature
- **Wu Wei** (non-action/effortless action)
- **Yin-Yang** and Feng Shui concepts
- *Tao Te Ching* — key text

**Legalism** (Han Feizi, Shang Yang, Xunzi):
- Laws, military power, authority
- People are prone to do bad → strict laws + punishment needed
- Used by Qin dynasty

**Buddhism in China:**
- Mahayana Buddhism introduced in Han period
- Chinese monks: **Faxian** (Han), **Xuanzang** (Tang)
- State religion under Tang Tai Zong & Empress Wu Zetian
- Major sects: Tiantai (study of Tripitaka → printing), Chan/Zen (meditation)
- Cave temples: **Dunhuang**, **Longmen**

### Intellectual & Economic Achievements
- **Silk Road** (opened by Han Wu Di) — trade network across Asia
- **Imperial Examination** — recruited qualified officials (practiced for 2,000 years)
- **Exports:** Porcelain, silk, tea, herbal medicine, paper, metal tools
- **Currency:** Coins, banknotes, paper credit, abacus
- **Navigation:** Compass, shipbuilding, Zheng He's maritime expeditions
- **The Four Great Inventions:** Paper, printing, gunpowder, compass (later periods)

### Artistic Achievements
- **Bronze metallurgy:** Shang/Zhou ritual vessels
- **Pottery:** From clay → terracotta → porcelain (Jingdezhen)
- **Jade carving:** Amulets, ornaments, ceremonial items
- **Silk weaving:** Status symbol; monopolized by court
- **Sculpture:** Terracotta Army (Qin), Longmen Buddha
- **Architecture:** Great Wall, Forbidden City, pagodas, Summer Palace
- **Painting:** Landscape scrolls (Song dynasty), black ink + brush on silk/paper
- **Calligraphy:** High art — ancient style (Shang/Zhou), classic style (Song)
- **Literature:** Five Classics, *Journey to the West*, *Romance of the Three Kingdoms*, *Dream of the Red Mansions*

### Chinese Society & Politics
- **Mandate of Heaven** — ruler as "Son of Heaven"
- Centralized vs. decentralized rule depending on ruler's power
- **Social hierarchy:** Emperor → court ministers → city governors → gentry → peasants
- Education & skills = means to climb social ladder (unlike India's fixed caste)
- **Ancestor worship** — filial piety extended to spirits

### Key Vocabulary — China
- **Mandate of Heaven:** Divine right to rule, can be revoked
- **Oracle bones:** Animal bones used for divination (Shang)
- **Feudalism:** Decentralized rule through appointed lords
- **Filial piety (Xiao):** Respect for parents/ancestors
- **Silk Road:** Overland trade route connecting China to the West
- **Terracotta Army:** Life-size clay soldiers in Qin Shi Huang's tomb
- **Hundred Schools of Thought:** Flourishing of philosophy (500 BC)
- **Yin-Yang:** Complementary cosmic forces
- **Gentry:** Literate, wealthy landowners in countryside
- **Pagoda:** Multi-storied Buddhist tower

## 7. Ancient Greece

### Geography
- Southern Europe (Balkan Peninsula)
- Mountainous land, archipelago
- Limited arable land
- Mediterranean Sea influence → maritime trade

### Peoples
- **Hellene** (Indo-European) — tribes: Aeolians, Dorians, Ionians, Achaeans
- Settlements from Greece to Spain (Iberia) and Turkey (Ionia)
- Way of life: fishing, seafaring, farming, animal domestication

### Timeline

| Period | Dates | Key Events |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Prehistoric** | ~7000–750 BC | Minoan (Crete — Sir Arthur Evans; Linear A); Mycenaean (mainland — Heinrich Schliemann); Dark Age (~750 BC — Dorian invasion, Homeric epics) |
| **Hellenic Period** | 750–338 BC | Authentic Greek culture |
| · Archaic Greece | 750–500 BC | Rise of city-states |
| · Classical Greece | 499–338 BC | **Golden Age of Athens**; Persian Wars; Peloponnesian War |
| **Hellenistic Period** | 336–31 BC | **Alexander the Great**; cultural fusion; early globalization |
| Roman Period | 31 BC–324 CE | |
| Byzantine Period | 4th–15th c. | Eastern Roman Empire |
| Ottoman Period | 15th–20th c. | Turkish rule |

### City-States: Athens vs. Sparta

| Aspect | **Athens** | **Sparta** |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Location** | Attica (east coast); strategic harbors | Laconia |
| **Government** | **Democracy** (direct; citizen assembly) | **Oligarchy** (dual monarchy; warrior class) |
| **Values** | Intellectualism, art, debate, individualism | Militarism, discipline, collectivism |
| **Economy** | Maritime trade (olive oil, wine, pottery) | Agrarian, subsistence, self-sufficient |
| **Society** | Slaves (~40% of pop.) allowed leisure for citizens; women excluded from politics | Spartiates (warriors), Perioikoi (foreigners), Helots (serfs); women had more rights |
| **Education** | Philosophy, arts, rhetoric | Military training from age 7 |
| **Army** | Powerful navy (trireme) | Hoplite phalanx; elite land army |
| **Key Statesmen** | Draco, Solon, Cleisthenes, Pericles | Leonidas (Thermopylae) |

### Athenian Democracy
- **Cleisthenes** — "Father of Athenian Democracy"
- **Institutions:** Ekklesia (citizen assembly), Boule (Council of 500), Prytany (50 leaders rotating), Strategoi (generals)
- **Direct democracy** — no representatives (but women, children, slaves excluded)
- **Voting at Pnyx**
- **Pericles** (495–429 BC) — Golden Age; built Parthenon; Delian League → imperialism → Peloponnesian War

### Major Incidents
1. **Persian Wars** (499–449 BC): Darius I at Marathon (490 BC); Xerxes at Thermopylae (Spartans) & Salamis (Athenian navy)
2. **Peloponnesian War** (431–404 BC): Athens vs. Sparta → decline of Hellenic period
3. **Macedonian Invasion** (338 BC): Philip II conquered Greece; **Alexander the Great** (336–323 BC) established empire from Greece to India

### Greek Religion
- **Polytheism, anthropomorphic gods** — Olympian gods: Zeus (sky), Hera (family), Athena (wisdom), Apollo (sun/music), Ares (war), Aphrodite (love), Poseidon (sea), Hades (underworld), etc.
- Temples as dwelling places of gods; oracles (e.g., Delphi)
- **Olympic Games** — held every 4 years at Olympia to honor Zeus

### Intellectual Achievements
- **Humanism** — admiration of human ability, dignity; reason over superstition
- **Philosophy:** Socrates, Plato, Aristotle (to be covered in later lecture)
- **Rationalism** — Greeks used reason and critical thinking unlike mysticism in other civilizations
- Excellent speakers (rhetoric) could climb social ladder

### Artistic Achievements

**Sculpture (3 styles):**
1. **Archaic:** Stiff, Egyptian-influenced; feet/arms close to body
2. **Hellenic (Classical):** Natural, calm expression, ideal beauty, nudity — e.g., Discobolus (Myron), Venus Braschi
3. **Hellenistic:** Intense movement/emotion — e.g., Laocoon, Jockey of Artemision

**Painting:** Mostly on pottery; Black-figure → Red-figure → Hellenistic (oriental influence)

**Architecture:**
- Post-and-lintel (trabeation) structure
- Columns (Doric, Ionic, Corinthian) supporting entablature & pediment
- **Parthenon** — Doric temple to Athena, commissioned by Pericles (architects: Iktinos, Kalikrates; sculptor: Phidias)
- **Erechtheum** — Porch of the Caryatids

**Literature:**
- **Homer:** *Iliad* (Trojan War) and *Odyssey* (Odysseus's voyage)
- **Drama:** Tragedy (Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides) and Comedy (Aristophanes, Menander)
- Greek amphitheaters built on slopes

**Music:** Lyre, panpipes; Aristotle's *Poetics*; ethos (cosmic rhythm — spiritual/emotional)

### Hellenistic Period (336–31 BC)
- Alexander the Great's conquest → fusion of Greek + Oriental cultures
- Spread of Greek language, art, ideas across Asia
- After Alexander's death: empire divided among Ptolemy (Egypt), Seleucus (Persia), Antigonus (Greece)
- Early globalization — trade across civilizations

### Key Vocabulary — Greece
- **Polis:** Greek city-state
- **Democracy (Demokratia):** Rule by the people
- **Oligarchy:** Rule by a few
- **Hoplite:** Greek infantry soldier
- **Phalanx:** Military formation of hoplites
- **Trireme:** Greek warship
- **Acropolis:** Elevated fortress/temple area
- **Agora:** Public marketplace / gathering place
- **Humanism:** Focus on human potential and reason
- **Anthropomorphic:** Gods in human form
- **Hubris:** Excessive pride (leads to downfall in tragedy)
- **Catharsis:** Emotional purification through tragedy
- **Hellenistic:** Greek-influenced cosmopolitan culture post-Alexander
