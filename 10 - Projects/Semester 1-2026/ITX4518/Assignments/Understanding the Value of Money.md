## Part 1: Money Characteristics Analysis — Robux (30 Points)

| # | Characteristic | In-Game (Roblox) | Real World |
|---|---------------|------------------|------------|
| 1 | **Durability** | ✅ **Pass** — Robux exists as digital data on Roblox servers. Cannot be physically destroyed, lost, or worn out. | ❌ **Fail** — Tied to Roblox's existence. If the platform shuts down, all Robux are lost. No real-world durability. |
| 2 | **Portability** | ✅ **Pass** — Accessible from any device with an internet connection. Can be earned and spent globally within Roblox. | ❌ **Fail** — Cannot be transferred or used outside the Roblox ecosystem. No physical form to carry. |
| 3 | **Divisibility** | ✅ **Pass** — Robux can be divided into small units (fractional amounts available depending on pricing tiers). | ⚠️ **Partial** — Can be divided in-game, but exchange rates with real currency create awkward conversion ratios. |
| 4 | **Uniformity** | ✅ **Pass** — All Robux units are identical in value: 1 Robux = 1 Robux within the game economy. | ❌ **Fail** — Different purchasing methods (direct buy vs. earned) have different effective exchange rates to real currency. |
| 5 | **Acceptability** | ✅ **Pass** — Widely accepted across millions of Roblox experiences, items, and avatar accessories. | ❌ **Fail** — No real-world merchant or service accepts Robux as payment. Zero real-world acceptability. |
| 6 | **Limited Supply** | ❌ **Fail** — Roblox Corporation creates new Robux and sells them. Supply is determined by demand, not a fixed limit. No scarcity mechanism. | ❌ **Fail** — Infinite potential supply means Robux cannot store value reliably compared to limited-supply assets. |

### Summary
Robux passes most characteristics **within the Roblox ecosystem** but fails nearly all of them **in the real world**. This makes Robux a functional in-game currency but not real money — it lacks the broad acceptability and independent store of value that define true currency.

## Part 2: Time Value of Money (TVM) — $10,000 Graduation Bonus (40 Points)

**Given:**
- Principal (PV) = **$10,000**
- Time (n) = **10 years**
- Inflation rate = **4% per year**
- Investment return = **8% per year**

---

### Option A: Cash Savings (0% interest, 4% inflation)

You keep \$10,000 in cash with 0% nominal interest. Inflation erodes purchasing power.

**Formula:** Future Purchasing Power = PV ÷ (1 + inflation rate)^n

**Calculation:**

| Step | Operation | Result |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| 1 | (1 + 0.04)^10 | = 1.04^10 |
| 2 | 1.04^10 | = **1.48024** |
| 3 | \$10,000 ÷ 1.48024 | = **\$6,755.64** |

**Result:** After 10 years, your \$10,000 in cash will have the same purchasing power as **\$6,755.64** today. You lost **\$3,244.36** (32.4%) of your purchasing power to inflation.

---

### Option B: Asset Investment (8% annual return)

You invest \$10,000 in an asset yielding 8% nominal return per year.

**Formula:** FV = PV × (1 + return rate)^n

**Calculation:**

| Step | Operation | Result |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| 1 | (1 + 0.08)^10 | = 1.08^10 |
| 2 | 1.08^10 | = **2.15892** |
| 3 | \$10,000 × 2.15892 | = **\$21,589.25** |

**Result:** After 10 years, your investment grows to **\$21,589.25** — more than double your original principal.

---

### Comparison

| Metric | Option A (Cash) | Option B (Invest) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Nominal Value (Year 10) | \$10,000 | \$21,589.25 |
| Real Purchasing Power (Year 10) | \$6,755.64 | — |
| Real Return (after 4% inflation) | -32.4% | +115.9% (nominal) |
| Risk Level | Very Low | Moderate |

### Key Insight
Cash feels safe but **guarantees a loss of purchasing power** over time due to inflation. Investing carries short-term risk but is the only way to grow wealth beyond inflation. This is the fundamental time value of money principle: a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow.
