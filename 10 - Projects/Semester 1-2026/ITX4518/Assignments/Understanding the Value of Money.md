## Part 1: Money Characteristics Analysis — V-Bucks (30 Points)

Evaluate V-Bucks (Fortnite's in-game currency) against the 6 characteristics of money.

| # | Characteristic | In-Game (Fortnite) | Real World |
|---|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | **Durability** | ✅ **Pass** — V-Bucks exist as digital data on Epic Games servers and cannot be physically destroyed, lost, or worn out. | ❌ **Fail** — Tied entirely to Fortnite's existence; if the game shuts down, all V-Bucks are lost instantly. |
| 2 | **Portability** | ✅ **Pass** — Accessible from any device logged into your Epic account globally within Fortnite. | ❌ **Fail** — Cannot be transferred, withdrawn, or used outside the Fortnite ecosystem. |
| 3 | **Divisibility** | ✅ **Pass** — Items have specific V-Buck prices and the Battle Pass rewards fractional amounts, allowing division. | ❌ **Fail** — Sold only in fixed bundle tiers (e.g., 1000 V-Bucks), not freely divisible like real currency. |
| 4 | **Uniformity** | ✅ **Pass** — Every V-Buck is identical in value: 1 V-Buck = 1 V-Buck across all Fortnite purchases. | ⚠️ **Partial** — Bundles have a fixed exchange rate to USD (e.g., 1000 V-Bucks = $7.99), but creator-coded content can affect perceived value. |
| 5 | **Acceptability** | ✅ **Pass** — Widely accepted for all Fortnite skins, emotes, battle passes, and item shop purchases. | ❌ **Fail** — Zero acceptance outside Fortnite; no real-world merchant accepts V-Bucks. |
| 6 | **Limited Supply** | ❌ **Fail** — Epic Games creates new V-Bucks and sells them on demand; there is no supply cap or scarcity mechanism. | ❌ **Fail** — Infinite potential supply means V-Bucks cannot reliably store value compared to limited-supply assets (e.g., Bitcoin, gold). |

### Summary
V-Bucks is a **functional in-game currency** but **not real money**. It passes all six characteristics within Fortnite's closed ecosystem but fails most of them in the real world — primarily because it lacks broad acceptability and an independent store of value.

## Part 2: Time Value of Money (TVM) Application (40 Points)

**Given:**
- Principal (PV) = **$10,000** (graduation bonus)
- Time (n) = **10 years**
- Inflation rate = **4% per year**
- Investment return = **8% per year (nominal)**

---

### Option A: Cash Savings (0% interest, 4% inflation)

> **Formula:** Future Purchasing Power = PV ÷ (1 + inflation rate)^n

| Step | Operation | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | (1 + 0.04) = 1.04 | |
| 2 | 1.04^10 | **1.48024** |
| 3 | $10,000 ÷ 1.48024 | **$6,755.64** |

**Answer:** After 10 years, the $10,000 in cash will have the same purchasing power as **$6,755.64** today. The purchasing power loss is **$3,244.36 (32.4%)** due to inflation.

*Full calculation: $10,000 / (1.04)^10 = $10,000 / 1.480244 = $6,755.64*

---

### Option B: Asset Investment (8% annual return)

> **Formula:** FV = PV × (1 + return rate)^n

| Step | Operation | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | (1 + 0.08) = 1.08 | |
| 2 | 1.08^10 | **2.15892** |
| 3 | $10,000 × 2.15892 | **$21,589.25** |

**Answer:** After 10 years, the investment grows to **$21,589.25** — more than double the original principal.

*Full calculation: $10,000 × (1.08)^10 = $10,000 × 2.158925 = $21,589.25*

---

### Comparison

| Metric | Option A (Cash) | Option B (Invest) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Nominal Value (Year 10) | $10,000 | $21,589.25 |
| Real Purchasing Power (Year 10) | $6,755.64 | — |
| Purchasing Power Change | **-32.4%** | **+115.9%** |
| Risk Level | Very Low | Moderate |
| Best For | Short-term safety (under 1 year) | Long-term wealth building |

### Key Insight

> A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow. Cash feels safe but **guarantees a loss** of purchasing power over time due to inflation. Investing carries short-term volatility but is the only way to grow wealth beyond inflation — the fundamental principle of the Time Value of Money.
